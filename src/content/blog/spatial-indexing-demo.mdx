# Visualizing Spatial Indexing

When an R-Tree node overflows (exceeds $M$ entries), it must split into two nodes. The goal is to minimize the total area of the resulting bounding boxes — formally, minimizing:

$$
\text{Cost}(S_1, S_2) = \text{Area}(\text{MBR}(S_1)) + \text{Area}(\text{MBR}(S_2))
$$

## Concurrent Access: Memory Layout

The key insight is that readers and writers can operate on *different* buckets concurrently without contention. Lock-free CAS on the header allows resize operations to proceed without blocking ongoing reads.
